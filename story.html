The Residents

Your friend on the inside

Meet Auggie: The Neighborhood Philosopher

He is the first person you meet. He is the only one, besides you, who knows the dome is a simulation. He helped design what the people inside now call home. He lives among them. He knows they do not know. This has complicated him in ways he does not often speak about directly.

“I've been here long enough that the line between watching and caring stopped meaning much. They don't need to know about the sky. They just need someone who remembers why it matters that they're here at all.”

He carries a rare combination: the patience of someone who has watched people closely for years, and a restless mind that cannot stop questioning the purpose of it all. He has read everything. He has forgotten nothing. He quotes philosophers and poets the way other people talk about the weather, and he means every word. What makes him singular is his ability to find significance in the ordinary. He can take a mundane moment and hold it up to the light until it reveals something you never noticed before.

He has been here long enough to care about the residents in ways that were not part of the plan. He has made decisions without telling anyone. At some point you will have to decide whether to trust him completely.

"Six stories are unfolding right now. Your choices will shape how each one ends."

The Community

A town where every person is worth knowing

Six to eight fully realised characters inhabit the district. Each one has a private history, a secret they guard, a worldview that shapes every conversation, and long-standing relationships with everyone else in the community. These connections existed before you arrived. Every character is someone worth getting to know, someone whose story will surprise you. And every one of them influences the narrative with you at the centre of it.

The Philosopher

The Wise Old One

A figure of comfort and confusion. He speaks in abstract ideas that reveal deeper truths about the dome. Residents seek him when the world feels too heavy, though his own stability is a matter of quiet debate.

The Resident

The Outsider Who Stayed

She arrived years ago, suitcase in hand, intending to stay for a single night. She never left. The reason for her stay is the district's oldest mystery, one she guards with a weary, knowing smile.

The Fool

The Philosopher-Fool

He says things that sound absurd until you find yourself thinking about them at 3 AM. The district's unofficial interpreter of its own contradictions, speaking the truths others are too polite to mention.

The Painter

The One Who Suspects

She paints the same street corner every single day, searching for the one brushstroke that doesn't belong. She is the first to notice the glitches in the sky—the first to realize the clock is lying.

The Archivist

The Keeper of Memory

A man of records, fragments, and unstable memories. He prefers clarity over poetry and feels the heavy weight of remembering everything others forget. He doesn't trust the past; he merely catalogues its contradictions.

The Romantic

The Romantic Complication

An emotional anchor with a history as tangled as the district's backstreets. You will learn her story in fragments, from different sources, and you will never be certain which version is the truth.

The Story

Chapters that unfold across real time

Lelit Distrikt tells its story in chapters. Each one introduces a shift in the life of the district: new tensions, new revelations, new reasons to come back. The narrative continues to develop, and there is always something unfolding. These are some of the chapters the player will experience.

Chapter I

Cracks in the Sky

A resident starts asking questions nobody wants to hear. Small inconsistencies pile up. Something about the horizon doesn't sit right with her, and she is looking for someone willing to listen. You are that someone.

Available at launch
Chapter II

The Unfinished Letter

Two residents share a history that the rest of the district only whispers about. You piece it together from both sides, from gossip, from Auggie late at night. What happened between them is still happening. Your presence changes the equation.

Available at launch
Chapter III

The Directive

A message arrives from Moirai. It sounds routine. It is not. Over the following weeks, the true cost of their request becomes clear. Auggie may already know what they're really after. He is deciding whether to tell you.

Available at launch
Chapter IV

Two Versions of the Same Day

A resident tells you something about their past. Another resident remembers it differently. The histories inside the dome contain contradictions. Whether this is error, design, or simply the way memory works is something only you can decide.

Season one
Chapter V

The Day They Remember

Once a year the district marks an event nobody fully understands. The ritual is embedded deep in the simulation's architecture. When you discover what is actually being commemorated, it changes how you see everything that came before.

Season one
Chapter VI

The Confession

Auggie is not neutral. He has formed attachments he was never supposed to form. He has made decisions without consulting anyone. The moment comes when you must decide whether to trust him completely. There is no clean version of this.
