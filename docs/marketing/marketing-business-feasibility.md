# Business Feasibility

> This document is intentionally qualitative. It evaluates whether the current product direction is commercially plausible, not whether a specific revenue model is already proven.

## Executive Summary

`Lelit Distrikt 2` is commercially plausible because it has three things many AI-game projects do not:

- a distinctive authored premise
- implemented local-first simulation systems
- a believable secondary market outside pure game sales

The project is still risky, but the main risk is not "can the core idea be built at all?" The repository already shows that a meaningful core has been built. The harder questions are positioning, focus, packaging, and whether the final product becomes more game, more platform, or a disciplined hybrid.

## Why The Project Is Feasible

### The product has a real technical center

There is already a live in-game conversation runtime, durable persistence, continuity logic, and a dedicated Studio tool. That makes the concept substantially more feasible than projects that are still depending on pitch language or mockups.

### Local-first design is commercially meaningful

Local inference changes the economics and the message:

- lower ongoing operating cost
- stronger privacy story
- reduced dependence on third-party API pricing
- easier positioning as a premium product instead of a subscription obligation

### The simulation is legible to more than one market

The same core system can be interesting to:

- narrative-game players
- AI-curious players
- immersive-installation partners
- studios exploring social NPC systems

That does not guarantee success, but it does widen the range of viable outcomes.

## Why The Project Is Still Hard

### A strong prototype can still fail as a product

Technical novelty does not solve discoverability, user onboarding, minimum-spec clarity, or content depth. Those remain serious commercial challenges.

### The message can become muddy very quickly

If the project is described simultaneously as a game, a platform, a general AI framework, a philosophical art piece, and an enterprise solution, buyers will struggle to understand what is actually being offered.

### Narrative quality remains non-negotiable

The AI layer can create attention, but retention and reputation will come from worldbuilding, tone, pacing, and character writing.

## Most Plausible Commercial Shapes

### Shape A: Premium niche narrative game

This is plausible if the team can turn the current systems into a focused authored experience with a clear cast and environment.

### Shape B: Showcase-plus-services model

This is plausible if the demonstrator is compelling enough to generate commissioned work, installations, or technical partnerships.

### Shape C: Internal framework that supports both

This is likely the most realistic near-term interpretation of the project: one codebase, one simulation backbone, multiple outward expressions.

## Feasibility Conditions

The project becomes meaningfully more feasible if the team does the following:

1. Reduce message drift between code reality and outward claims.
2. Produce a polished demonstrator that clearly shows continuity, not just dialogue.
3. Narrow the first commercial offer instead of trying to sell every possible future at once.
4. Keep the authored setting and characters as first-class product assets, not wrappers around the AI tech.

## Bottom Line

`Lelit Distrikt 2` is feasible, but only under disciplined framing. Its strength is not generic "AI NPCs." Its strength is a local-first social simulation architecture inside a distinctive narrative world. If that is communicated clearly and productized carefully, the project has credible commercial paths.
