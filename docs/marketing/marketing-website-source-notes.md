## What The Website Can Safely Claim Today

Safe present-tense claims:

- local-first NPC conversation
- Unreal Engine 5.7 foundation
- persistent transcript, memory, summary, and action storage
- a continuity engine that can propagate information between NPCs
- a browser-based Studio tool for inspection and identity editing
- real-time sky synchronization to local time

Safe directional claims:

- deeper behavioral coupling between memory and world behavior
- further tuning of synthesis and propagation quality
- expansion from prototype systems into a fuller authored game experience

## What The Website Should Not Claim As Current

Do not present these as active unless revalidated in code and technical docs:

- full consumer-grade voice experience as the main shipped interaction model
- production-ready multimodal character vision
- generalized "characters see everything you do" claims
- mobile companion messaging as a live product feature
- fully complete, consumer-ready autonomous society simulation

## Tone Guidance

The best tone is:

- confident
- literary
- technically grounded
- specific without being breathless

Avoid:

- generic "revolutionary AI" language
- inflated claims that collapse prototype and release state
- feature soup that makes the product harder to understand

## Positioning Guidance By Page Type

- `Home page`: lead with the world, the premise, and the memory thesis.
- `Experience page`: explain what it feels like to inhabit the district.
- `Tech page`: explain persistence, continuity, local inference, and Studio observability.
- `Pitch or partner pages`: emphasize that the systems are real and inspectable, not just conceptual.
