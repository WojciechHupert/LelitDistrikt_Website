# Business Plan

> Rewritten against the active project baseline on May 6, 2026.

## Business Framing

`Lelit Distrikt 2` should currently be treated as a product platform in transition:

- today: a technically credible local-first social simulation prototype
- next: a polished showcase and partner-facing demonstration
- later: either a commercial narrative game, a licensable simulation framework, or both

The plan works best when it does not pretend those stages are already the same thing.

## The Product Opportunity

The project combines three attractive qualities:

- strong narrative and worldbuilding potential
- concrete AI systems that already exist in code
- local-first infrastructure that avoids per-interaction API cost

That gives it two plausible business paths:

1. `Audience path`
Develop the prototype into a distinctive premium narrative game.

2. `Capability path`
Package parts of the stack as bespoke installations, demonstrations, or licensable social-simulation technology.

The right plan keeps both open until the public response and production realities become clearer.

## Practical Three-Stage Plan

### Stage 1. Product Demonstrator

The immediate business objective is not "ship the full game." It is to produce a demonstrator that makes the existing systems legible and impressive to outsiders.

That demonstrator should emphasize:

- one strong environment
- a small but memorable social circle
- visible persistent memory
- believable continuity between interactions
- Studio-side inspectability for partners, press, or collaborators

Success at this stage is attention, credibility, and proof of differentiation.

### Stage 2. Partner And Commissioned Work

If the demonstrator resonates, the next stage is selective commercial work that monetizes the stack without derailing the core product.

Good targets include:

- cultural or exhibition installs
- interactive brand experiences
- bespoke AI-character demonstrations
- consulting or prototyping work for teams exploring local NPC systems

The value proposition is not "generic chatbot integration." It is:

`a local-first character-and-memory simulation stack with observability and authored identity control`

This work should be carefully scoped. The goal is to fund the core product and generate case studies, not to become a full-time agency by accident.

### Stage 3. Premium Game Release

The consumer product path remains strong if the team can turn the prototype's systems into a focused, coherent narrative game.

The commercial release should lean on:

- a sharply defined district and cast
- consequence through memory and rumor
- privacy-first local execution as part of the brand
- high-quality authored atmosphere around the AI systems

## Competitive Advantages

### Local-first economics

Inference is designed around the player's own machine and local services. That changes cost structure materially compared with permanently cloud-metered designs.

### Stronger-than-average technical credibility

The project already has runtime persistence, continuity logic, and a control surface. That is much more persuasive than concept-only AI game pitches.

### A better narrative framing than generic "AI NPCs"

The product is not only about smart characters. It is about a district with memory, leakage, hearsay, and social consequence.

### Observability

`Moirai Studio Pro` is a meaningful asset. Teams and partners trust systems they can inspect.

## Principal Risks

### Scope creep

The project can easily become too many things at once: game, platform, toolset, installation, AI showcase. Business discipline will matter as much as technical ambition.

### Product-message drift

Older materials overstate some features. If public messaging gets ahead of the build, trust erodes.

### Prototype-to-product gap

A sophisticated prototype is not the same thing as a stable consumer release. Packaging, onboarding, hardware expectations, and content completeness remain major work.

### Opportunity cost from services work

Partner revenue can help, but too much custom work can consume the time required to finish the core product.

## Immediate Strategic Priorities

1. Produce one marketing-safe demonstrator narrative that cleanly shows conversation, persistence, and continuity.
2. Tighten the product language so every current-state claim is defensible.
3. Decide which outward path gets priority first: consumer showcase, partner showcase, or hybrid showcase.
4. Treat `Moirai Studio Pro` as part of the product story rather than an internal afterthought.

## Bottom Line

The business case is strongest when `Lelit Distrikt 2` is positioned as a differentiated local-first social simulation with real systems already working. The near-term objective is not maximum breadth. It is a sharply presented proof that this world can remember, that those memories matter, and that the resulting product can become both culturally distinctive and commercially useful.
