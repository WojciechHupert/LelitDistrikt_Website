### What Lelit Distrikt is about

Lelit Distrikt is a sealed world, a social experiment preserved beneath a dome. The outside world collapsed, but they were never told.

The project is an exploration of people, observation, and the weight of memory. It is a precise, intentional construction that creates a specific emotional experience. You aren't here to solve a puzzle or win a war; you are here to witness the texture of human life that was almost lost.

The town looks real, the characters feel real, and the sky above is a projection of a normality that no longer exists.

### Why this world exists

The Enclosure was built to preserve a record of how we lived before: our relationships, routines, conflicts, and small daily kindnesses. It is simultaneously an archive, an experiment, and a memorial.

The dome protects more than just people; it protects an "archive quality" of life. It preserves ways of living and routines rather than just plot events. It is a transition from a world that moved on to something post-human, back to something embodied, local, and finite.

### The player's role

#### The Observer

You stand on the outside of their reality, watching the patterns of a society that believes its horizon is real. Your gaze is not neutral; to observe is to curate.

#### The Participant

By engaging with the residents, you become part of their world. You form genuine connections across a moral asymmetry that only you and Auggie understand.

#### The Witness

You are an anthropologist of the ordinary. You gather stories, notice the unsaid, and hold the weight of truths that the residents cannot yet bear.

#### The Interpreter

You decide what to report to Moirai and what to keep as a secret. You define the value of these simulated lives through your own attention.

### The district as a stage

The town of Lelit is more than a backdrop; it is a protagonist. The central square is the informal social infrastructure where everyone passes through and every story converges.

Inspired by the ensemble model of great television, the district has its own rhythm. Cafes open with the sun and the piazza fills with the evening light. It is a Mediterranean social space where the atmosphere itself tells a story of a world frozen in a perfect, messy normality.

## `experience.html`

### Header

- The Experience

### The Core

#### What if the world was an experiment?

Imagine entering a place where everything feels real, but you know it's a design. A Mediterranean town frozen in time, filled with people who go about their lives, unaware that they are part of a simulation. Lelit Distrikt doesn't just tell you this; it lets you live it.

You aren't just playing a game; you are stepping into a social study. You are the observer, the guest, and perhaps the only one who knows the truth about the sky above. The experience asks you to consider what makes a person real when their world is built by architects you've never met.

### What to Expect

#### A world that listens and remembers.

##### Talk like you normally do

Forget choosing from a list of pre-written options. In Lelit, you speak or type exactly what you want to say. The people here understand you and respond naturally, guided by their own personalities and whatever is happening in the moment.

##### They remember your words

Characters don't just forget you when you walk away. They remember what you told them, how you made them feel, and even share gossip with their neighbors. Your reputation grows and shifts based on your actual conversations over time.

##### Life goes on without you

The town follows its own rhythm. People go to the market, meet at the cafe, and return home to sleep, even when you aren't there to watch. The world doesn't pause; it keeps moving, ensuring things have changed every time you return.

##### Your friend in the system

You aren't alone. Auggie is your constant companion and the only one who knows the world is a simulation. He's your guide, your confidant, and someone you can rely on to tell you what's really happening beneath the surface.

##### Contact beyond the screen

Lelit Distrikt is more than just a window on your monitor. Characters like Auggie can reach out to you through phone messages, keeping you connected to the story as you go about your real-world day.

##### Entirely private and yours

Your conversations and experiences are yours alone. Everything happens right on your computer. Nothing is sent to the internet or stored on a distant server. It's a private world that stays with you, for you.

### The Oversight

#### The Architects: Moirai

Moirai is a name the residents never hear. To them, the world is just the world. To you, Moirai is the organization that built this place, the invisible hand that keeps the simulation running and the "sky" working correctly.

They are the administrators of this experiment. They are clinical, efficient, and always watching. They see the town not as a home, but as a system to be preserved. They provide the directives you follow, but their true motives remain hidden behind formal memos and cold instructions.

Working for Moirai means you are part of the machinery. You are here to observe and interact, but as you grow closer to the people of the district, you might start to wonder if the architects are watching you just as closely as they watch their creation.

#### Origin of Moirai

"The threads of life are woven long before they are cut."

### Outside the Dome

#### A world that doesn't wait

Most games stop the moment you close them. Lelit Distrikt is different. The simulation keeps running, the people keep living, and the town evolves while you're away. You aren't the center of this world; you are a visitor to a place that has its own life, its own schedule, and its own secrets.

Auggie is your connection to what you missed. Because he's the only one who knows the truth, he might message you to tell you about a conversation he overheard or a strange event in the square. It's not a simple notification; it's a letter from a friend who is still inside the experiment while you are back in your real life.

The Moirai Group, overseeing the experiment, might send you formal notes or memos. Their requests always seem simple, but they remind you that the architects are always keeping track of your progress.

You can message Auggie back whenever you want. Ask him how he's doing, or tell him what to look out for. He responds in real-time, proving that your influence on the district doesn't end just because the game isn't open.

## `story.html`

### Header

- The Residents

### Your friend on the inside

#### Meet Auggie: The Neighborhood Philosopher

He is the first person you meet. He is the only one, besides you, who knows the dome is a simulation. He helped design what the people inside now call home. He lives among them. He knows they do not know. This has complicated him in ways he does not often speak about directly.

"I've been here long enough that the line between watching and caring stopped meaning much. They don't need to know about the sky. They just need someone who remembers why it matters that they're here at all."

He carries a rare combination: the patience of someone who has watched people closely for years, and a restless mind that cannot stop questioning the purpose of it all. He has read everything. He has forgotten nothing. He quotes philosophers and poets the way other people talk about the weather, and he means every word. What makes him singular is his ability to find significance in the ordinary. He can take a mundane moment and hold it up to the light until it reveals something you never noticed before.

He has been here long enough to care about the residents in ways that were not part of the plan. He has made decisions without telling anyone. At some point you will have to decide whether to trust him completely.

### Interstitial Quote

"Six stories are unfolding right now. Your choices will shape how each one ends."

### The Community

#### A town where every person is worth knowing

Six to eight fully realised characters inhabit the district. Each one has a private history, a secret they guard, a worldview that shapes every conversation, and long-standing relationships with everyone else in the community. These connections existed before you arrived. Every character is someone worth getting to know, someone whose story will surprise you. And every one of them influences the narrative with you at the centre of it.

#### The Philosopher

- Role: The Wise Old One

A figure of comfort and confusion. He speaks in abstract ideas that reveal deeper truths about the dome. Residents seek him when the world feels too heavy, though his own stability is a matter of quiet debate.

#### The Resident

- Role: The Outsider Who Stayed

She arrived years ago, suitcase in hand, intending to stay for a single night. She never left. The reason for her stay is the district's oldest mystery, one she guards with a weary, knowing smile.

#### The Fool

- Role: The Philosopher-Fool

He says things that sound absurd until you find yourself thinking about them at 3 AM. The district's unofficial interpreter of its own contradictions, speaking the truths others are too polite to mention.

#### The Painter

- Role: The One Who Suspects

She paints the same street corner every single day, searching for the one brushstroke that doesn't belong. She is the first to notice the glitches in the sky, the first to realize the clock is lying.

#### The Archivist

- Role: The Keeper of Memory

A man of records, fragments, and unstable memories. He prefers clarity over poetry and feels the heavy weight of remembering everything others forget. He doesn't trust the past; he merely catalogues its contradictions.

#### The Romantic

- Role: The Romantic Complication

An emotional anchor with a history as tangled as the district's backstreets. You will learn her story in fragments, from different sources, and you will never be certain which version is the truth.

### The Story

#### Chapters that unfold across real time

Lelit Distrikt tells its story in chapters. Each one introduces a shift in the life of the district: new tensions, new revelations, new reasons to come back. The narrative continues to develop, and there is always something unfolding. These are some of the chapters the player will experience.

##### Chapter I: Cracks in the Sky

A resident starts asking questions nobody wants to hear. Small inconsistencies pile up. Something about the horizon doesn't sit right with her, and she is looking for someone willing to listen. You are that someone.

- Status: Available at launch

##### Chapter II: The Unfinished Letter

Two residents share a history that the rest of the district only whispers about. You piece it together from both sides, from gossip, from Auggie late at night. What happened between them is still happening. Your presence changes the equation.

- Status: Available at launch

##### Chapter III: The Directive

A message arrives from Moirai. It sounds routine. It is not. Over the following weeks, the true cost of their request becomes clear. Auggie may already know what they're really after. He is deciding whether to tell you.

- Status: Available at launch

##### Chapter IV: Two Versions of the Same Day

A resident tells you something about their past. Another resident remembers it differently. The histories inside the dome contain contradictions. Whether this is error, design, or simply the way memory works is something only you can decide.

- Status: Season one

##### Chapter V: The Day They Remember

Once a year the district marks an event nobody fully understands. The ritual is embedded deep in the simulation's architecture. When you discover what is actually being commemorated, it changes how you see everything that came before.

- Status: Season one

##### Chapter VI: The Confession

Auggie is not neutral. He has formed attachments he was never supposed to form. He has made decisions without consulting anyone. The moment comes when you must decide whether to trust him completely. There is no clean version of this.

- Status: Season one

### Core System

#### Moirai: The Continuity Orchestration System.

Built on Unreal Engine 5.7 with a native C++ runtime, Moirai is the canonical state authority for time, memory, and world continuity. It enforces a strict separation between System Truth (the immutable record of every interaction) and Character Perception (the subjective, emotionally biased recall each NPC experiences). Every conversation is processed through a recursive pipeline that extracts bulleted facts, identifies emotional weight, and propagates knowledge autonomously through a 10-hop social graph.

### Architecture Diagram Labels

#### SYSTEM.IN [01] - Player I/O

Interaction capture via custom C++ frameworks. Handles asynchronous input sanitization and logic routing.

- STATUS: BUFFERED // TYPE: RAW_TEXT

#### LOGIC.INF [02] - Local Brain

Private inference using optimized local language models. High-fidelity reasoning with zero-latency context injection via the Moirai bridge.

- ARCH: LOCAL // MODE: PRIVATE_INF // TELEMETRY: MONITORED

#### MOIRAI.CORE [03] - Continuity

Recursive fact extraction, autonomous action scheduling, and world state synthesis. Manages the Social Graph and 10-hop Propagation buffers.

- LANG: C++ // ENGINE: UE 5.7 // LOOP: AUTONOMOUS

#### SYSTEM.OUT [04] - NPC Recall

Emotional bias injection and temporal decay filters. Dynamic response synthesis based on active relationship tensors.

- FILTER: BIAS_TENSOR // TARGET: UI_WIDGET

### Data Stores

#### System Truth

Secure local storage for transcripts and structured memory records (UTC Synchronized)

#### Social Graph

Propagation events // 10-Hop Social Chains // Relationship proximity weighting

#### Recall Filter

Human-like degradation // Emotional bias injection

### Deep-Dive Cards

#### Dual-Layer Memory Model

Moirai enforces a split-brain architecture. The Transcripts record maintains an append-only, high-fidelity log of every interaction. The Memory Records store structured FACT: and EXCHANGE: reports extracted by the system. When an NPC recalls information, it passes through a Recall Filter that introduces temporal decay and emotional weighting.

- Tags: C++, SQLite, WAL-Mode

#### Relationship Tensors

Trust, intimacy, and shared history are not binary flags. Lelit Distrikt models relationships as dynamic tensors that influence every response. What you say to one character ripples through the Social Graph, propagated by gossiping NPCs during off-screen synthesis cycles. The world remembers how you treat its residents, and they speak to each other when you aren't around.

- Tags: Social Graph, 10-Hop Propagation

#### Autonomous Narrative Cycle

The simulation operates independently of the player. Managed by a dedicated background thread, NPCs meet, reflect, and share information on a deterministic clock. Every social exchange creates a lasting ripple in the simulation, ensuring the district continues to evolve in real-time.

- Tags: Recursive Synthesis, Autonomous Clock
