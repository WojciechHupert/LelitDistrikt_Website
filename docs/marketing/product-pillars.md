# Product Pillars (v3.3 Update)

## 1. Conversation As Gameplay

Conversation is not there to decorate a quest log. It is one of the project's central mechanics. The player learns about the district, forms impressions of its residents, and changes what the simulation knows through dialogue.

## 2. Memory As World State

In many games, dialogue disappears the moment it is spoken. Here, selected facts become durable Neural Records. This turns conversation from a disposable moment into a state-changing event that shapes the future of the district.

## 3. Autonomous Social Evolution

The important unit is not the isolated NPC. It is the district as an autonomous social network. Information spread through 10-hop chains means the world continues to evolve, leak secrets, and form rumors even when the player is offline.

## 4. Local-First Privacy And Control

The project's inference and persistence stack (Ollama, Kokoro, Whisper) is designed for 100% local operation. This is both a technical and philosophical choice, ensuring total data privacy and zero dependency on cloud subscriptions.

## 5. Glass-Box Observability

Moirai Studio Pro gives developers and collaborators a way to inspect the district's "neural vitals." With real-time high-visibility charts and inference logging, AI behavior is no longer a black box but a transparent, debuggable system.

## 6. Atmosphere With Recursive Systems

The Mediterranean sealed-district aesthetic provides emotional coherence. Beneath the photorealistic mediterranean lighting (Nanite/Lumen) lies a complex recursive system where every social exchange creates a lasting ripple in the simulation.
