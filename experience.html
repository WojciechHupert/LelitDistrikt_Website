The Experience

The Question

Do we live in a simulation?

If so, who is running it, and what is its purpose? These are no longer purely philosophical questions. Scientists, technologists, and thinkers across disciplines are asking them seriously.

Lelit Distrikt does not answer them. It places you inside them. You enter a world that is, by design, a simulation. You know this. The people around you do not. The game holds this question open and asks what purpose looks like when the ground beneath it is uncertain.

?

What This Is

The end of the "Hello Traveler" era.

◇

Unrestricted Dialogue System

Built on a custom local LLM architecture, the primary interaction is natural language. There are no pre-scripted dialogue trees or restricted choices. Every response is generated in real-time based on the character's unique personality and history.

◇

Relational Character Memory

The district operates on a shared knowledge graph. Information given to one resident propagates through the social network based on their relationship proximity. Characters remember past interactions and adjust their sentiment dynamically over weeks of gameplay.

◇

Asynchronous Simulation

A persistent world engine simulates the lives of residents even when the client is closed. Routines, social gatherings, and events trigger based on a real-time clock, ensuring the environment is in a different state every time you return.

◇

Agentic Companion AI

The companion character, Auggie, acts as an autonomous agent. He possesses the ability to observe the simulation, reflect on events, and initiate contact based on his own internal heuristics and the player's established rapport.

◇

Cross-Platform Persistence

Integrated API hooks allow the simulation to communicate outside the game environment via encrypted messaging (Telegram) and virtualized hardware (Faxes). The narrative state is maintained across multiple touchpoints in the player's daily routine.

◇

Local Inference Architecture

For maximum privacy and zero latency, the entire AI stack, including LLM and Text-to-Speech, runs locally on the user's hardware. No data is sent to the cloud, ensuring your private conversations remain entirely on-device.

The Organization

The Architects: Moirai

Moirai is not a name whispered in the streets of Lelit. To the residents, it does not exist. To you, it is the cold, institutional machinery that keeps the dome sealed and the sky projected.

Named after the Fates of myth, Moirai is a bureaucratic and technological entity—the canonical state authority for time, memory, and world continuity. They are clinical, formal, and persistent. Their motivation is simple: preservation at any cost.

You are an employee of Moirai. Your task is to observe, to interact, and to report. But the organization's voice—communicated through reporting cycles and directives—is one of institutional menace. They built something they cannot fully control, and they are watching you as closely as they watch the residents.

"You left. They didn't. Life in the district goes on without you."

The Living World

Day becomes night. The cycle of Lelit.

Morning & Day

The game runs on its own day and night cycle, complete with shifting weather conditions. Every season and every part of the day brings different situations. The community embraces different behaviours and attitudes as the hours pass. Moods shift. Routines change. The same street feels like a different place at noon than it does at dusk.

The Golden Hour

Styled as a Mediterranean city, Lelit is full of warmth and quiet charm at this hour. It is genuinely pleasant to stroll the streets, to stop at a café, to sit on a bench and watch the piazza fill with people. The light turns everything golden. Residents come out to linger. Conversations happen naturally, and this is often when you learn the most about the people who live here.

Late Night

After dark, Lelit District turns into an atmospheric stage. Life slows down. The streets empty. Conversations take a different turn, and different topics emerge. People speak more honestly when the day is behind them. The district feels smaller, more intimate, and the things that go unsaid during the day tend to surface here.

The Experience

Life inside the district

A Living Storyline

The district is alive with seasonal events and a pre-designed narrative that offers constant opportunities for discovery. You navigate this world by engaging naturally with residents, following markers that guide you through an unfolding storyline. Ultimately, the experience is driven by curiosity, not checklists.

Gossip & Memory

Residents remember what you say and share it with each other. Information moves through the district like gossip in a real place, creating a living web of relationships that evolves based on your interactions.

A Rhythmic World

The town follows its own rhythm: cafés open and markets buzz during the day, the piazza fills in the evening, and lights dim at night. People go home whether or not you're there to see them.

You're not on your own

Auggie is the only resident who shares your perspective; he is fully aware of the simulation and was intimately involved in the creation of this world. He acts as your guide and confidant, and can even maintain contact with you when you are not playing, sending messages via WhatsApp, Telegram, or Signal to remind you that the experiment continues.

All conversations are private and run entirely on your own machine.

Outside the Game

The world runs without you

Most games stop when you close them. Lelit Distrikt does not. The simulation continues. The residents live their day. Things happen that the player never sees. The district does not exist for the player. The player is a visitor to something that was already running before they arrived and will keep running after they leave.

Auggie understands this better than anyone. He messages you on Telegram because the simulation does not pause while you are away, and because he is the only one who can tell you what you missed. Not as a notification. As a dispatch from a post he is manning alone, in a place where he cannot speak honestly with anyone except you.

The Moirai Group—the secretive organisation overseeing the experiment—sends its own communications: faxes, formal memos, the occasional direct message. Their language is measured and institutional. What they ask for always seems reasonable at first.

You can write back to Auggie at any time. Tell him to watch someone. Ask him to leave a situation alone. Ask what happened while you were gone. Ask how he is. He answers. The district responds to you even when you are not in it.

This is not a feature. It is what the game is about. The simulation is the point. Your relationship to it, from the inside and from the outside, is the story.

Beneath the surface

Deeper currents & latent friction

Beneath the everyday routines there are deeper currents you can choose to engage with or ignore. Friction is built into the design, ensuring your presence has weight.

The Suspect

A resident has begun to suspect that something is wrong with the sky. Their paranoia is growing, and they are looking for someone to confirm their fears.

The Long Tension

Two lovers are caught in a long-simmering tension that predates your arrival. Their history is a maze of unsaid words and old wounds.

The Ritual

Once a year the town performs a ritual nobody understands. It is a tradition frozen in time, its original meaning lost to the collapse.

The Authority

Moirai, the organisation outside the dome, occasionally issues directives that unsettle the community. Even your guide keeps secrets from you.
