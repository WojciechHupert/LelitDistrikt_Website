The Experience

The Core

What if the world was an experiment?

Imagine entering a place where everything feels real, but you know it’s a design. A Mediterranean town frozen in time, filled with people who go about their lives, unaware that they are part of a simulation. Lelit Distrikt doesn't just tell you this—it lets you live it.

You aren't just playing a game; you are stepping into a social study. You are the observer, the guest, and perhaps the only one who knows the truth about the sky above. The experience asks you to consider what makes a person real when their world is built by architects you've never met.

?

What to Expect

A world that listens and remembers.

◇

Talk like you normally do

Forget choosing from a list of pre-written options. In Lelit, you speak or type exactly what you want to say. The people here understand you and respond naturally, guided by their own personalities and whatever is happening in the moment.

◇

They remember your words

Characters don't just forget you when you walk away. They remember what you told them, how you made them feel, and even share gossip with their neighbors. Your reputation grows and shifts based on your actual conversations over time.

◇

Life goes on without you

The town follows its own rhythm. People go to the market, meet at the café, and return home to sleep—even when you aren't there to watch. The world doesn't pause; it keeps moving, ensuring things have changed every time you return.

◇

Your friend in the system

You aren't alone. Auggie is your constant companion and the only one who knows the world is a simulation. He’s your guide, your confidant, and someone you can rely on to tell you what's really happening beneath the surface.

◇

Contact beyond the screen

Lelit Distrikt is more than just a window on your monitor. Characters like Auggie can reach out to you through phone messages or even faxes, keeping you connected to the story as you go about your real-world day.

◇

Entirely private and yours

Your conversations and experiences are yours alone. Everything happens right on your computer—nothing is sent to the internet or stored on a distant server. It’s a private world that stays with you, for you.

The Oversight

The Architects: Moirai

Moirai is a name the residents never hear. To them, the world is just the world. To you, Moirai is the organization that built this place, the invisible hand that keeps the simulation running and the "sky" working correctly.

They are the administrators of this experiment—clinical, efficient, and always watching. They see the town not as a home, but as a system to be preserved. They provide the directives you follow, but their true motives remain hidden behind formal memos and cold instructions.

Working for Moirai means you are part of the machinery. You are here to observe and interact, but as you grow closer to the people of the district, you might start to wonder if the architects are watching you just as closely as they watch their creation.

"You left. They didn't. Life in the district goes on without you."

A Living World

Day becomes night. The rhythm of Lelit.

Morning & Day

The town wakes up with the sun. You’ll see the streets fill with people heading to work or the market. The air is bright, the mood is industrious, and the community is busy with the small tasks of daily life. It’s the perfect time to meet everyone and see the district at its most energetic.

The Golden Hour

As the sun begins to set, the town takes on a soft, golden glow. This is when the piazza fills with residents looking to relax. People linger over coffee, children play in the square, and the atmosphere turns social and warm. It’s the best time for long, meaningful conversations on a park bench.

Late Night

When darkness falls, Lelit becomes quiet and atmospheric. The crowds disappear, and the streets grow still. In the silence of the night, people often speak more honestly. You might find residents sharing secrets they would never mention during the day, revealing the deeper truths of life inside the dome.

The Feeling

What it's like to be there

A Story You Can Touch

The district isn't just a backdrop; it’s a living story that unfolds around you. You explore by talking to people and following your own curiosity. There are no checklists—just a world full of secrets waiting for you to find them.

Gossip Travels Fast

If you tell a secret to one person, don't be surprised if someone else brings it up later. People talk, and information moves through the town just like it does in a real community. Your actions have real social consequences.

A Natural Flow

The town follows a natural schedule. Markets open and close, the evening square buzzes with life, and eventually, the lights go out. People aren't waiting for you to arrive; they are busy living their own lives.

You Have a Direct Line

Auggie is your bridge between worlds. He’s the only one who knows what you know. He stays in touch even when you're away, sending you updates on his phone to let you know that life in the district continues, and that he's still there.

A private experience that runs entirely on your own device.

Outside the Dome

A world that doesn't wait

Most games stop the moment you close them. Lelit Distrikt is different. The simulation keeps running, the people keep living, and the town evolves while you’re away. You aren't the center of this world; you are a visitor to a place that has its own life, its own schedule, and its own secrets.

Auggie is your connection to what you missed. Because he’s the only one who knows the truth, he might message you to tell you about a conversation he overheard or a strange event in the square. It’s not a simple notification—it’s a letter from a friend who is still inside the experiment while you are back in your real life.

Even the Moirai Group—the organization running the study—might send you formal notes or memos. Their requests always seem simple, but they remind you that the architects are always keeping track of your progress.

You can message Auggie back whenever you want. Ask him how he's doing, or tell him what to look out for. He responds in real-time, proving that your influence on the district doesn't end just because the game isn't open.

Secrets to Find

Deeper stories & hidden truths

Beneath the calm routines of the town, there are hidden stories you can choose to explore. Every interaction has weight, and every secret you uncover changes how you see the district.

The Doubter

One resident has started to notice things that don't make sense. They are beginning to question the very world around them and are looking for someone to trust.

The Old Grudge

A long-standing tension between two neighbors has existed since long before you arrived. Their shared history is a tangle of old arguments and unspoken feelings.

The Town Tradition

Once a year, the residents perform a mysterious ritual. It’s a tradition that has been passed down for generations, though its original meaning has long been forgotten.

The Hidden Eye

The organization outside the dome occasionally sends instructions that can be unsettling. Even your closest guide might be keeping a few secrets from you.
